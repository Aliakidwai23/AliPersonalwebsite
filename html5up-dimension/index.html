Intro

Hello everyone, my name is Ali Kidwai. I was born in Orlando, Florida, in 2000. Since childhood, I have been an avid video gamer, which sparked my interest in anything to do with computers. I first learned some programs like Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Animate, and Maya. I explored my artistic side with these programs, but during my senior year in high school, I became interested in computer science. I took a class and later pursued computer science as my career. I am currently a full-time student at the University of Houston, pursuing my computer science degree. In my free time, I am committed to continuous learning, often working on projects or learning about new things in the field. Recently, I have been learning about machine learning, which has also sparked an interest in data science.

Future plans

I am currently learning machine learning, but I plan to dig deeper into data science. Some of the things I have planned to work on soon include a couple of machine learning projects, starting to learn data mining, working on a few projects to help me understand data mining better, and creating useful projects. Additionally, I plan to work on many short AI projects using Python, such as a Discord bot and a face recognition app. These are my short-term goals that I hope to accomplish within a month or two.

I am currently in a seven-week fellowship with Headstarter, where I will be working on many projects to improve my existing skills and learn new ones. I also have plans to learn a bit about video game development, as I wish to explore as many aspects of computer science as possible. I believe game development would teach me many new things, such as working with a game engine, and allow me to use programs I first learned years ago, such as Maya and Photoshop. Video game projects would also be a blast to work on.

In the long term, my goal is to pursue studies in data science and hopefully land an internship and a job in that specific field. However, computer science is always evolving with new demands and emerging fields, so I try to stay open-minded and explore different options.
